Board-level cyber resilience briefing

Sportetitive’s ransomware attack should be understood as a company-wide management failure, not only a technical incident.

The attack affected operations, customer trust, employee data, supplier assurance, legal compliance and recovery decision-making. This website brings the key findings together so the board and senior management can understand what happened, who owns what, and what must improve next.

Why this matters

Sportetitive was compromised through social engineering, supplier trust and weak identity controls. The attackers captured privileged credentials, moved across systems, stole personal data and then encrypted files to disrupt the business. This made the incident operational, financial, legal and reputational at the same time.

The response therefore needs to connect technical recovery with leadership accountability, staff wellbeing, insurer coordination, regulatory reporting, supplier governance and long-term resilience planning.

What this website covers

  • A clear summary of the attack path, business disruption and data exposure.
  • An incident response structure showing what must happen from detection to lessons learned.
  • A leadership view of responsibilities across the CEO, CIO, CTO, CFO, CHRO, NEDs, IT team and communications roles.
  • Framework-based analysis using MITRE ATT&CK and Zero Trust.
  • Strategic recommendations for board-level improvement.

Board-level priorities

01

Understand how the attack happened and why supplier trust, missing MFA and privileged access became critical weaknesses.

02

Clarify role ownership across the board and senior management during incident response and recovery.

03

Use recognised frameworks to justify stronger controls, governance and investment decisions.

04

Translate lessons learned into practical improvements in monitoring, access control, backups, supplier assurance and board reporting.
