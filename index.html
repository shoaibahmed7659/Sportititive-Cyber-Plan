What happened — the attack in brief

The attack exploited social engineering, supplier trust, and missing identity controls to escalate a single phishing email into a business-wide crisis.

6 Jan 2026

Pete Mitchell (MSP technician) publicly posts about charity work with Sportetitive on LinkedIn, sharing his email address. Attackers collect this intelligence and plan a targeted approach.

19–20 Feb 2026

Pete receives a convincing spear-phishing email posing as the Sportetitive CEO. He clicks the link, and his credentials are captured. Attackers log in remotely that night via MSP remote access.

23 Feb 2026

A keylogger deployed on the network captures the IT Manager's domain administrator credentials. Attackers gain full access to all infrastructure and cloud services.

8 Mar 2026, 20:23

Ransomware deployed. Web services, IMS, and core databases go offline. Files encrypted using AES-256, RSA and Curve25519 (Conti variant). Six stores closed.

9 Mar 2026, 07:40

Ransom demand sent via the compromised CEO email account. Attackers demand 1.3 BTC within 72 hours or stolen data will be published.

18 Mar 2026

Forensic specialists confirm 60,000+ customer records and all employee records exfiltrated. ICO formally notified under the Data Protection Act 2018, s.108. ICO investigation opened.
