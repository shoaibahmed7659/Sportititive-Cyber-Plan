Strategic actions

What Sportetitive should improve next

The organisation should move beyond immediate recovery and build a stronger resilience model. These recommendations focus on the weaknesses exposed by the incident and the governance improvements needed to reduce repeat risk.

Identity

Strengthen identity security

Implement MFA across email, cloud, VPN, privileged accounts, backups, and supplier access. Separate admin accounts, reduce standing privilege, and review access rights regularly.

Suppliers

Improve supplier governance

Require stronger supplier access controls, clearer contractual obligations, monitoring expectations, incident notification duties, and regular assurance reviews.

Legacy risk

Address the POS constraint

Treat the legacy POS platform as a board-level risk. Use compensating controls in the short term and develop a replacement or redesign roadmap in the medium term.

Visibility

Increase monitoring and detection

Expand logging, alerting, and endpoint visibility so unusual logins, privilege changes, tool disablement, and suspicious data movement can be identified earlier.

Recovery

Strengthen backup resilience

Maintain segregated, tested, and trusted backups, supported by documented recovery priorities and regular restore exercises.

Governance

Improve board exercises and review

Run tabletop exercises, board reviews, and lessons-learned sessions regularly so cyber risk stays visible on the risk register and in board reporting.
