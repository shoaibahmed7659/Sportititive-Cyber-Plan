Resilience Improvement Plan

From crisis response to lasting resilience: Sportetitive's three-phase improvement plan

The ransomware attack exposed specific, correctable gaps in identity management, supplier governance, monitoring, legacy infrastructure, and organisational preparedness. This improvement plan is structured across three phases — stabilise, improve, and transform — and prioritises the highest-risk items first. Every recommendation is practical, assigned to an owner, and aligned to either NCSC, ICO, or NIST guidance.

Cyber Essentials NCSC 10 Steps ICO accountability guidance NCSC Zero Trust
Phase 1

Stabilise — Months 1 to 3

Close the gaps that made the attack possible and that leave Sportetitive exposed to the same attack vector today. These actions cannot wait.

Critical

Enforce MFA on every external access route

Multi-factor authentication on every account that can be reached from the internet — Microsoft 365, Azure AD, VPN, MSP remote management, backup portals, and cloud administration consoles. This is the single most impactful control because the entire Sportetitive attack was enabled by credentials alone. MFA would not have prevented the phishing click, but it would have stopped the attacker from using Pete Mitchell's credentials to log in.

Owner: CTO + IT ManagerNCSC MFA guidance
Critical

Revoke and rebuild all compromised credentials and access routes

Reset all administrator, service account, backup portal, cloud, MSP, email, and POS support credentials. Implement Privileged Access Workstations (PAWs) for administrator tasks. Eliminate standing domain-level access for all external parties. Implement just-in-time access for supplier accounts with time-limited, logged sessions.

Owner: CTOMITRE M1026 Privileged Account Management
Critical

Establish segregated, immutable, tested backups

Sportetitive's online backup credentials were compromised because they were shared with the domain administrator account. Immediately separate all backup credentials from domain admin accounts. Implement the 3-2-1-1-0 backup rule: three copies, two media types, one offsite, one offline, zero errors confirmed by test restoration. Run a restoration test before the current incident is closed.

Owner: CTO + IT ManagerNCSC offline backups guidance
High

Introduce 24/7 monitoring and alerting

Deploy a Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) system with behavioural rules that alert on off-hours admin logins, lateral movement via RDP, large outbound data transfers, new admin account creation, and changes to backup access. The Sportetitive attacker spent 13 days inside the network undetected. A SIEM with a managed SOC (Security Operations Centre) or an EDR with a 24/7 alert-response service would have triggered alerts within hours of the initial login.

Owner: CIO + CTONCSC logging guidance
High

Revise MSP contract and governance model

Spherical Services requires a revised contractual framework including: defined and documented access scope, named individuals with background-checked credentials, mandatory MFA for all access, time-limited session-based access, full logging shared with Sportetitive in real time, a right-to-audit clause, mandatory incident notification within 2 hours, and annual security assessments. The existing contract almost certainly does not include these provisions.

Owner: CIO + CFONCSC supply chain security
High

Deliver phishing awareness training across the entire organisation

Social engineering was the entry point. Every employee, including senior management and suppliers with Sportetitive access, must complete phishing awareness training with regular simulated phishing tests. The training must be adapted to Sportetitive's context — including the risks of posting operational details on professional social media. Simulated tests should be run quarterly.

Owner: CHRO + IT ManagerNCSC phishing guidance
Phase 2

Improve — Months 3 to 12

Systematic improvements to reduce attack surface, increase visibility, and build resilience across the organisation.

Planned

Network segmentation across all sites and the POS environment

The Sportetitive attack moved laterally from a single compromised account to every part of the network because all systems were on a flat network. Implement network segmentation to isolate: the corporate network, store POS systems, administrative systems, customer data environments, and supplier/MSP access zones. A breach in one zone should not propagate to others without crossing a monitored control point.

Owner: CTONCSC segmentation guidance
Planned

Achieve and maintain Cyber Essentials Plus certification

Cyber Essentials is the UK government's baseline certification scheme for cyber security. Achieving Cyber Essentials Plus demonstrates to customers, suppliers, the ICO, and insurers that Sportetitive has addressed the five technical controls that prevent the majority of commodity cyber attacks. It also reduces cyber insurance premiums and is increasingly required by public sector and enterprise B2B partners.

Owner: CIONCSC Cyber Essentials overview
Planned

POS legacy constraint: transition to SphereSales v5 or alternative

SphereSales v4 runs on Windows Server 2012 R2, which reached end of Extended Security Updates in October 2023 and no longer receives security patches. This creates ongoing vulnerability exposure that cannot be fully mitigated without patching. The CTO must present a business case for transition to SphereSales v5 or a cloud-native POS alternative within the next 12 months. Until migration, enhanced segmentation and compensating controls are required.

Owner: CTO + CFONCSC patching guidance
Planned

Data minimisation and access-based data governance

Under UK GDPR Article 5, personal data must be adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary for the purpose for which it is processed. Sportetitive's customer data exposure covered 60,000+ records. A data mapping exercise should identify what is held, where, how long, and whether it is still required. Retention policies, automated deletion, and access controls at the data level must be implemented.

Owner: CIO + NED ComplianceICO data minimisation guidance
Phase 3

Transform — Year 2 onwards

Build a security culture and architecture that makes Sportetitive structurally harder to attack, not just better at responding after the fact.

Strategic

Implement Zero Trust Architecture

Move the entire organisation towards a Zero Trust model: verify every access request explicitly, enforce least privilege on every account and service, assume breach in system design, and verify devices before granting access. This is a multi-year programme but starts immediately with MFA enforcement and network segmentation (Phases 1 and 2). The NCSC publishes a Zero Trust architecture guide for UK organisations.

Owner: CIO + CTONCSC Zero Trust guidance
Strategic

Establish a cyber resilience governance structure

Cyber security must be a standing item on every board meeting, not a reactive topic that appears only after incidents. The board should receive a quarterly cyber risk dashboard covering: threat landscape, control effectiveness, incident metrics, staff training completion, and improvement plan progress. The CIO presents. The NED compliance provides independent challenge. A formal Information Security Management System (ISMS) aligned to ISO 27001 should be scoped for Year 2.

Owner: CIO + NED ComplianceNCSC Board Toolkit
Strategic

Annual cyber resilience exercises — tabletop and simulated attack

An incident response plan that has never been tested is a hypothesis. Run a tabletop exercise annually where the leadership team works through a simulated ransomware scenario against the playbook. Every gap found in an exercise is a gap that would have cost money, regulatory exposure, or reputation in a real attack. Engage the NCSC's Exercise in a Box service — a free resource for UK organisations.

Owner: CIONCSC Exercise in a Box (free)

Board resilience metrics — what to track

Cyber resilience improvement must be measurable. These are the metrics the board should receive quarterly to confirm progress is being made and risks are reducing.

MFA coverage

Percentage of accounts with external access that have MFA enforced. Target: 100% within 90 days.

Phishing simulation pass rate

Percentage of staff who correctly identify and report simulated phishing. Target: >90% within 12 months.

Patching compliance

Percentage of critical patches applied within 14 days of release. Target: 95%+. SphereSales v4 EOL tracked as a board risk item.

Backup test success rate

Percentage of scheduled backup restoration tests that succeed. Target: 100%. Frequency: quarterly.

Incident response time

Time from detection to containment in simulated exercises. Target: under 4 hours for SEV-1 by end of Year 1.

Supplier security assessments

Percentage of suppliers with network access who have completed a security assessment in the past 12 months. Target: 100% annually.
