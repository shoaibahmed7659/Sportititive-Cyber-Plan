Leadership & Role Ownership

Who owns what in a cyber incident at Sportetitive

Cyber incidents fail at the handover points — where no single person believed they were responsible. This page makes ownership explicit across every leadership role and every phase of response. No responsibility should fall through the gaps. All roles, all phases, all decisions, assigned.

Executive ownership matrix

Every function in a cyber crisis is assigned to a specific role. Multiple roles are listed for shared responsibility areas. “Accountable” means the decision rests with that role. “Responsible” means that role does the work. “Consulted” means that role's input is required. “Informed” means that role must be kept updated.

Role profiles — what each leader must do

Chief Executive Officer (CEO)

First action within 30 minutes of SEV-1 declaration.

Ultimate accountability for the crisis response rests with the CEO. The CEO decides on store closures and reopening, approves all external communications and customer notifications, confirms the no-ransom policy with the board, and manages stakeholder relationships including suppliers, investors, and media. All external statements go through the CEO.

Chief Information Officer (CIO)

First action within 15 minutes of SEV-1 declaration.

The CIO coordinates the overall incident response. They own the ICO notification obligation, manage the relationship with the cyber insurer and forensic providers, brief the board on technical developments in plain language, and are accountable for the personal data breach assessment. The CIO is the bridge between technical response (CTO) and business and regulatory decisions (CEO, NED, CFO).

Chief Technology Officer (CTO)

First action: immediately on confirmation of incident.

The CTO leads all technical response actions. Containment, isolation, forensic evidence preservation, and recovery sequencing all fall under the CTO's ownership. The CTO is accountable for the system-impact map, for confirming backup integrity, for managing the forensic provider relationship, and for making the recovery technically safe before any system is reconnected.

Chief Financial Officer (CFO)

First action within 1 hour of SEV-1 declaration.

The CFO activates the cyber insurance policy and manages the insurer relationship. They own cost logging from the moment the incident begins, report the incident to Action Fraud to obtain a crime reference number, manage emergency budget approvals for response expenditure, and provide the board with financial impact reporting throughout the incident.

Chief HR Officer (CHRO)

First action within 2 hours of SEV-1 declaration.

The CHRO is responsible for all employee welfare decisions during the crisis. They manage the notification to employees that their personal data may have been breached, ensure IT staff receive rest and welfare support during extended response periods, coordinate working arrangements, and advise on communications to staff. The CHRO also manages the employment relationship with any staff whose accounts or actions may require investigation.

Non-Executive Director — Compliance

Involved from ICO notification preparation stage.

The NED compliance role provides independent oversight of the regulatory response. They review the ICO notification before submission, advise on the risk assessment for individual customer notification, ensure board decisions are made and recorded in a legally defensible manner, and challenge the CIO on whether the response meets the legal obligations under the Data Protection Act 2018 and UK GDPR.

IT Manager

First responder — immediate technical execution role.

The IT Manager is the hands-on technical first responder. They execute the technical containment actions under the CTO's direction: isolating systems, preserving logs, disabling compromised accounts, blocking malicious traffic, confirming backup status, and briefing the CTO continuously. The IT Manager is the primary operational point of contact for forensic providers and the MSP suspension.

Spherical Services (MSP)

Access suspended immediately at Hour 0–1. Reinstate with controls only when confirmed safe.

The MSP's access is suspended at the start of the incident because the attack entered through MSP credentials. Before any access is restored, the MSP must confirm their own systems are clean, adopt a new monitored and time-limited access method with full logging, and accept a formal supplier security review. The MSP is a critical partner but must operate under tighter contractual security obligations going forward.

Decisions made without an incident log are decisions that cannot be defended.

Every decision, every instruction, every escalation during a cyber incident must be documented in real time with the decision-maker's name, the time, and the reasoning. The incident log is the primary evidence for the ICO investigation, the insurance claim, and the post-incident review. It is a legal document from the moment it is started.
