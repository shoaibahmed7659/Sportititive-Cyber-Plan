Senior management responsibilities

Cyber resilience depends on shared ownership across leadership

No single executive can manage a ransomware crisis alone. The Sportetitive scenario shows that response quality depends on clear ownership across business leadership, technical leadership, compliance, HR, finance, communications and external partners.

CEO

Chairs the executive crisis group, approves major operational and public decisions, and ensures management priorities remain aligned with the wider business response.

CIO

Owns business impact assessment, board reporting, recovery priorities, supplier governance and strategic decision logging. The CIO helps translate technical risk into business risk.

CTO

Leads technical containment, forensic coordination, eradication and restoration sequencing. The CTO ensures the technical response is controlled and evidence-based.

IT Manager

Executes technical actions, supports forensic investigation, preserves logs and coordinates the internal IT team during the response effort.

CFO

Handles insurer engagement, cost tracking, emergency spending, procurement and financial impact reporting. This role matters because major incidents create fast-moving cost and risk decisions.

CHRO / HR leadership

Supports staff communication, fatigue management, rota planning, wellbeing and human-factor controls such as awareness and social engineering resilience.

NED compliance / data protection

Supports breach assessment, regulatory reasoning, evidence logging, affected-individual risk assessment and formal notification where required.

Communications lead

Controls internal, customer, supplier and media messaging so communication is accurate, timely and consistent during a high-pressure incident.

External partners

Insurers, forensic specialists, police, legal advisers and the MSP all play defined supporting roles, but they should operate under monitored, approved and clearly documented arrangements.
