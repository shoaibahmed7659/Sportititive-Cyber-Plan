Shared ownership

Cyber resilience depends on clear leadership ownership across the organisation

No single executive can manage a ransomware crisis alone. Response quality depends on clear ownership across business leadership, technical leadership, compliance, HR, finance, communications, and external partners.

CEO

Leads business direction

Chairs the executive crisis group, approves major operational and public decisions, and keeps management priorities aligned with the wider business response.

CIO

Owns business risk translation

Owns impact assessment, board reporting, recovery priorities, supplier governance, and strategic decision logging so technical risk becomes business risk insight.

CTO

Leads technical response

Directs containment, forensic coordination, eradication, and restoration sequencing so the technical response remains controlled and evidence-based.

IT Manager

Executes operational actions

Supports forensic investigation, preserves logs, and coordinates the internal IT team under intense response pressure.

CFO

Controls cost and insurer action

Handles insurer engagement, cost tracking, emergency spending, procurement, and financial impact reporting during fast-moving decisions.

CHRO / HR

Protects people and resilience

Supports staff communication, fatigue management, rota planning, wellbeing, and human-factor controls such as awareness and social engineering resilience.

NED compliance

Supports regulatory reasoning

Helps with breach assessment, evidence logging, affected-individual risk assessment, and notification decisions where required.

Communications

Owns message discipline

Controls internal, customer, supplier, and media messaging so updates remain accurate, timely, and consistent during a high-pressure incident.

External partners

Support under governance

Insurers, forensic specialists, police, legal advisers, and the MSP play defined supporting roles but should operate under monitored, approved, and documented arrangements.
