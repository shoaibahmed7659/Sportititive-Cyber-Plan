Attack path and business effect

How the incident escalated from phishing and trust abuse into a board-level crisis

The attackers did not need a highly advanced entry technique. They succeeded by combining social engineering, supplier access, weak identity control, privileged account exposure and delayed detection. That chain turned a single compromise into widespread business disruption and a major personal data breach.

Initial targeting

The attackers used publicly available information and supplier relationships to craft a believable approach to an MSP technician. This shows how external trust relationships can become internal risk when there is not enough verification around who is contacting whom and why.

Credential compromise

Malware deployed through the phishing path later captured highly privileged credentials. This created a major escalation point because one powerful account gave the attackers broad access to systems, cloud services and core infrastructure.

Lateral movement

Once inside, the attackers explored the environment, disabled protections and moved across systems without being stopped early. Weak visibility and over-trusted access made it easier for the threat to spread before the business fully understood what was happening.

Operational disruption

Files were encrypted, the website and inventory management system became unavailable and online trading was interrupted. Store operations were also affected by uncertainty and executive decision-making pressure, even where some POS services remained operational.

Data breach impact

Investigators later confirmed that personal data relating to at least 60,000 customers and all employees had been exposed. This changed the event from a technical outage into a serious legal and reputational issue requiring formal breach assessment and regulatory reporting.

Business consequences

The attack created direct recovery costs, staff fatigue, insurer involvement, reputational harm, disrupted operations and fear of regulatory scrutiny. It also revealed structural weaknesses in supplier governance, privileged access design and the legacy POS environment.
