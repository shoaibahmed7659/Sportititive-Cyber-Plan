Framework-based analysis

MITRE ATT&CK explains the attack path, while Zero Trust explains the control response

MITRE ATT&CK helps the board understand how the attackers moved from initial contact to business impact. Zero Trust then provides a practical control narrative built on strong authentication, least privilege, segmentation, monitoring and supplier verification.

Reconnaissance and initial access

The attackers used public information and a convincing phishing route through a supplier relationship. The response lesson is that external trust should never replace proper verification.

Credential access

Captured privileged credentials allowed the attackers to avoid repeated intrusion effort. The control lesson is that identity should be strongly protected through MFA, admin separation and access review.

Lateral movement

Broad access and insufficient segmentation allowed the attackers to move further than they should have. Zero Trust reduces this by limiting what users, devices and suppliers can reach.

Exfiltration and impact

The theft of personal data and encryption of systems created a dual crisis of availability and legal exposure. The control response includes better monitoring, data protection, backup resilience and incident readiness.
