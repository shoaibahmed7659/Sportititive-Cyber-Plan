From social engineering to business impact.

Zero Trust model

Replace inherited trust with verified access.

Zero Trust gives the CIO a business-friendly control narrative: every user, device, supplier session and administrative action must be verified, limited and monitored.

  • Verify users, devices, MSP access and administrative sessions before access is granted.
  • Apply least privilege and role-based access so one compromised account cannot create a business-wide failure.
  • Segment POS, stores, HQ, cloud, backups and supplier access into controlled zones.
  • Monitor continuously for out-of-hours access, privilege escalation, AV disablement and unusual data movement.
Control families

Controls organised for implementation.

These control groups make the framework easier to convert into budget, technical tasks and governance reporting.

Identity

MFA and privileged access

Protect cloud, admin, VPN and MSP access with strong authentication and separate admin accounts.

Network

Segmentation

Separate POS, store, HQ, backup, supplier and cloud zones so compromise cannot spread freely.

Monitoring

Detection and alerting

Centralise logs and watch for unusual authentication, privilege escalation and data transfer behaviour.

Resilience

Immutable backups

Protect backup credentials, test restoration and define the recovery order before a crisis begins.

Supplier risk

MSP assurance

Review contracts, access scope, security controls, escalation paths and evidence obligations.

People

Awareness and reporting

Train staff and suppliers to recognise phishing, suspicious domains and unusual requests.
