Incident Response Plan & 72-Hour Playbook

What Sportetitive does in the first 72 hours of a ransomware attack

This is the operational guide for all employees and senior management. If Sportetitive suspects or confirms a ransomware attack, data breach, or major system outage, this page is the single reference point. Every role, every hour, every decision — in order. Aligned with NIST SP 800-61, NCSC ransomware guidance, ICO breach-reporting obligations, and the UK Data Protection Act 2018, section 108.

Under active attack? Jump to Hour 0–1. Do not pay any ransom. Do not wipe systems before forensics. Report: Action Fraud 0300 123 2040.
NCSC ransomware guidance NIST SP 800-61r2 ICO breach notification DPA 2018 s.108

When to activate this plan

This plan is activated immediately — not after discussion — if any single trigger below is observed.

Ransomware indicators

  • Files renamed with unknown extensions
  • Ransom note files appearing on desktops or shared drives
  • Systems unavailable or unresponsive without explanation
  • Web services suddenly offline
  • Unusual mass file changes across the network

Suspected data theft

  • Attacker claims to hold personal data
  • Large or unusual outbound transfers in network logs
  • Customer or employee data found outside approved systems
  • Unexplained access to database servers at unusual times

Account or supplier compromise

  • Admin account login from unexpected location
  • Senior mailbox accessed without the person's knowledge
  • MSP remote access during unauthorised hours
  • MFA prompt received without attempting login

Critical service outage

  • IMS unavailable or not updating
  • Website unreachable without a known cause
  • POS tills not connecting to central inventory
  • Backup platform inaccessible

Severity levels

SEV-1 Critical

Major business disruption. Confirmed ransomware. Confirmed or likely personal-data exposure. Loss of multiple critical systems.

Escalate to: CEO, CIO, CTO, CFO, CHRO, NED compliance, cyber insurer, forensic provider, Police, ICO assessment.
SEV-2 High

Serious compromise with limited spread. Credible attacker access but not yet enterprise-wide. MSP compromise, privileged account misuse.

Escalate to: CIO, CTO, IT Manager, NED compliance. Insurer notified.
SEV-3 Medium

Contained incident. No confirmed personal-data exposure or operational disruption. Isolated malware, contained phishing click.

Escalate to: CTO and IT Manager. CIO informed.
SEV-4 Low

Suspicious activity requiring investigation. No confirmed compromise. Suspicious email, failed login spike, blocked attachment.

Action: IT Manager triages and records. No external escalation required.

Hour 48 – 72

ICO submission & controlled recovery start

1
Identity & secure admin

No other system is restored until admin access routes are completely clean and MFA is enforced on all privileged accounts.

2
Network core, firewall & centralised logging

Monitoring must be operational before any system reconnects to the network.

3
Customer & employee data stores

Integrity validation and access-control verification required before use. Forensic sign-off mandatory.

4
Inventory Management System (IMS)

Stock replenishment and operational continuity depend on IMS.

5
Website & online trading

Customer-facing systems only after security validation. Rushed restoration risks re-infection.

6
Store systems & POS integration

POS reconnection only when both central systems and POS segment are confirmed safe.

7
Reporting & lower-priority systems

Analytics and non-essential tools last, after critical services are fully stable.

Board decision guide

These decisions must be made explicitly by the board during the first 72 hours and recorded in the incident log with full reasoning.

Should stores close?
Close only where safety cannot be confirmed. If POS and store systems are segregated from compromised corporate systems, stores should operate. Closure when unnecessary adds financial harm. Reopen as soon as CTO and CIO confirm safety.
Should the ransom be paid?
No payment by default. NCSC and NCA are clear: payment does not guarantee decryption, does not delete stolen data, and funds further criminal activity. Any exception requires board approval, insurer sign-off, and documented legal advice.
Should we contact the attackers?
Only through approved channels. No individual should contact attackers directly. Any contact goes through the insurer's breach coach, legal advisers, or law-enforcement-supported channels.
When do we notify customers?
Once personal-data breach is confirmed. Do not wait for absolute certainty if delay increases harm. Customer notification should be honest, specific about what is known, and include what customers should do.
Should systems be wiped immediately?
No. Preserve forensic evidence first. Wiping systems before forensic capture destroys the record needed for police, insurance, ICO, and lessons-learned purposes.
Can Spherical Services access systems?
Only with strict controls. MSP access remains suspended until a new monitored, time-limited access method is established with full logging. MSP systems must be confirmed clean before access is restored.
